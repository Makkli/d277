City Facts

Population

As of 2022, the United States Census Bureau reports the population of Tacoma, Washington was 221,776 people.1

Year of Incorporation

Tacoma was officially incorporated as a city on November 12, 1875.2

Region of the State

Tacoma is located in the western region of the state of Washington. It is situated along the shores of Puget Sound, south of Seattle. Many locals refer to the city and surrounding area as South Sound.

Classification

Tacoma is classified as an urban city.1 It's a major port city and an important center for business, culture, and education in the region.

Average Income Level

As of 2022, the United States Census Bureau reports the median household income in Tacoma was $69,9561, while the median household income for the entire state of Washington was $82,4003.

Did You Know?

Tacoma is home to the world's largest hot shop glassblowing facility aptly named the Museum of Glass.
